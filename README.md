# 🏋️ Gym Progress Tracker

A premium, mobile-first fitness tracking app for people who actually lift. Built to feel fast during a workout and genuinely useful outside of one — not just another gym log with a spreadsheet feel.

**[Live App →](https://vapp13.github.io/Gym-Progress-Tracker/)**

---

## What it does

Gym Progress Tracker covers the full loop of a training life: planning workouts, logging them in real time, tracking bodyweight and measurements, setting real goals, and watching progress accumulate — all in one installable, offline-friendly, dark-themed app.

### 🏠 Dashboard
A single home screen that actually tells you something useful the moment you open it: today's suggested workout (with one-tap Start), your active goal's progress, this week's training consistency, your current streak, personal records, recent sessions, and body metrics — no digging required.

### 💪 Workout Plans & Free Workouts
Build structured plans — exercises, target sets/reps/weight, rest periods, supersets, per-exercise notes, even specific training days — or skip planning entirely and jump into a **Free Workout**, building the session on the fly. Both work identically once you're training: same live logging experience, same history, same analytics.

### ⏱️ Live Workout Sessions
This is where the app is built to disappear. While training:
- **Continuous autosave** — close the app mid-workout, reopen it, and pick up exactly where you left off, timer included.
- **See your previous performance** for every exercise before you log a set, so progressive overload isn't a guessing game.
- **Log sets fast** — reps and weight start empty (no deleting a default "0" first), with warmup/drop/failure/assisted set types one tap away.
- **Automatic rest timer** between sets.
- **Add or remove exercises and sets mid-session** — plans are a starting point, not a cage.
- Pause and resume, or discard a session cleanly if you need to bail.

### 📊 Exercise Library
Every exercise with clear instructions, tips, and muscle breakdowns. Filter by muscle group (grouped by upper body / lower body / other), equipment, difficulty, or specific main/supporting muscles — search stays fast even as your library grows.

### 🎯 Goals
Set a real, specific goal — Lose Weight, Build Muscle, Improve Strength, Improve Endurance, and seven more types — each with the right fields for what it's actually tracking. One goal is always your "active" focus, driving your dashboard and weekly targets. Every goal gets its own dashboard: percent complete, time remaining, estimated completion, and a trend indicator so you know if you're on pace or falling behind.

### 📈 Progress Tracking
Track body weight plus 16 other measurements (body composition metrics like body fat % and BMI, and circumference measurements from waist to calves) — with full edit and delete history, because a single mistyped entry shouldn't wreck your graph. BMI and BMR calculate automatically from your profile, no manual math. Exercise-specific progress charts show strength and training volume over time, alongside your all-time personal records.

### 🏆 Personal Records
Heaviest weight, estimated 1-rep max, best reps, and best session volume — tracked automatically per exercise, only ever updated when you actually beat them.

### 👥 Friends (foundation)
Add friends via a shareable Friend Code, see their streaks and progress at a glance. Built with privacy first — no public directory of users, and sensitive health data (weight, measurements) is never shared, ever.

### 📱 Installable & Offline-Ready
A full Progressive Web App — install it to your home screen, and the interface loads even without a connection.

---

## Design Philosophy

- **Fast during training.** Every workout-screen decision optimizes for logging a set with the fewest taps and the least visual noise.
- **Dark by default**, built for gym lighting.
- **Nothing generic.** Set types, goal types, and measurements all map to real training concepts, not a one-size-fits-all number field.
- **Your data stays useful.** Nothing here calculates a metric once and forgets it — personal records, streaks, and progress all recompute live from your actual training history.
